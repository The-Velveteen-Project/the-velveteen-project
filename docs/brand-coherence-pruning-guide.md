# Brand Coherence & Pruning Guide

## Purpose
This guide exists to align Velveteen product sites without forcing visual uniformity. The objective is to prune each product site so they feel like part of the same intellectual family without losing their distinct aesthetic identity or domain-specific needs.

## Shared Family Traits
All Velveteen sites (parent and product) must inherently share:
- **Sober technical tone:** High signal, low noise.
- **Anti-hype posture:** Refusal to rely on "magic" AI glitter.
- **Founder-led credibility:** Honest scale, no fabricated enterprise facades.
- **Premium restraint:** Deliberate use of whitespace, refined typography, and glass-like, shadowless borders.
- **Clear hierarchy:** Primary actions and semantic sections are unmistakable.
- **Evidence before spectacle:** Information and architectural structure precede animation or decoration.
- **International readability:** Fluid, semantic HTML anticipating multilinguality.

## Product-Specific Identity
Products should not be clones. They serve different strategic functions:
- **The Velveteen Project:** The parent lab, the intellectual entry point, the manifesto. Focuses on overarching lab philosophy and system architecture.
- **EcoAgent:** Operational, applied, a system-in-motion. It addresses real-time mapping and immediate risks.
- **StochastoGreen:** Quantitative, austere, research-grade. It models heavy financial transition risk and behaves like a financial instrument.

## What to Prune
When auditing product sites, the following must be relentlessly softened, removed, or avoided:
- **Generic SaaS tropes:** 3D blobs, purple gradients, "Trusted by 10,000+" fake counters.
- **Fake enterprise cues:** Empty client logo marquees or aggressive sales funnels.
- **Noisy sci-fi effects:** Hyper-fast animations, matrix-code rain, or anything that turns the UI into a theatrical set piece.
- **Inflated copy:** Vague marketing claims without mathematical or architectural backing.
- **UI patterns that break family coherence:** Out-of-palette neons, heavy drop shadows, or rounded bubble buttons.

## What to Preserve
The following must be protected, as they allow the product its necessary domain variance:
- **Product-specific metaphor:** Let EcoAgent feel slightly more cartographic/dynamic; let StochastoGreen feel like a quantitative terminal.
- **Unique credibility signals:** Keep the domain-specific data visualizations, math, and stochastic modeling outputs.
- **Technical specificity:** Do not dumb down the domain terminology just to make it match the parent site's generalized tone.
- **Appropriate density:** If StochastoGreen requires a denser UI for data reading, permit it, provided it retains typographic restraint.

## Audit Lens for Future Product Passes
Use this checklist when reviewing product implementations:
1. What feels in-family? (Does it share the sober, technical tone?)
2. What feels too far from the parent brand? (Are there flashy outliers?)
3. What should be pruned? (Identify specific hype tropes to delete.)
4. What should remain product-specific? (Identify core domain aesthetics to protect.)
5. What improves coherence without flattening identity? (Can we sync fonts or button shapes while keeping the core layout distinct?)
